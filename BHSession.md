## “Tell me about yourself” (STAR-style but conversational)
            S – Situation
            I recently graduated and joined Cognizant as a Programmer Analyst Trainee, transitioning from an academic environment to a corporate one.
            T – Task
            My goal was to build strong technical fundamentals while adapting to professional expectations like teamwork, communication, and timelines.
            A – Action
            I actively participated in training programs, practiced coding consistently, clarified doubts with mentors, and collaborated with peers on assignments and mini-projects. I also focused on improving my communication and documentation skills.
            R – Result
            As a result, I became comfortable with the corporate workflow, improved my technical understanding, and developed confidence in working within a team-based IT environment.
            ✅ Wrap-up line:
            “I’m a motivated learner who enjoys solving problems and continuously improving, and I’m eager to contribute to real-world projects.”

## “Can you explain a technical issue you handled recently?”
            During my training at Cognizant, I was working on a testing project, and the automation test scripts were not running correctly.
            At that stage, my responsibility was to identify the root cause of the issue and ensure that the project worked as expected.
            The main challenge was to determine where the issue existed, whether it was related to locators, syntax errors, or script logic.
            I analyzed the test scripts line by line, verified all the locators, and checked for basic syntax and logical errors.
            I took the initiative to correct the faulty locators and fix syntax issues in the automation scripts.
            As a result, the project started running smoothly, and the test cases executed successfully.
            This experience strengthened my understanding of testing processes, defect analysis, and the importance of attention to detail in testing projects.

## “Please share an experience you had handling an upset customer”
            (You can use an internal client, teammate, or simulated customer scenario)
            S – Situation
            During a group project, one of the stakeholders (or team members acting as a client) was unhappy because the delivered functionality didn’t match expectations.
            T – Task
            I needed to address the concern, understand the issue clearly, and ensure the solution met their needs.
            A – Action
            I listened patiently without interrupting, acknowledged their concern, and asked clarifying questions. I then explained the misunderstanding, proposed a revised solution, and worked extra time with the team to implement the changes.
            R – Result
            The stakeholder was satisfied with the updated solution, tensions were reduced, and the project was successfully delivered on time.
            ✅ Key takeaway:
            “I learned that empathy, clear communication, and quick action are crucial when dealing with upset customers.”

## “How did you adapt to Cognizant’s environment, which is different from college life and your city?”
            S – Situation
            Moving from college life and my hometown to a corporate environment in Pune at Cognizant was a big change for me.
            T – Task
            I needed to quickly adapt to professional culture, structured schedules, formal communication, and a new city.
            A – Action
            I followed organizational guidelines, observed how seniors communicated, managed my time strictly, and became proactive in asking questions. Outside work, I adjusted to the city by networking with colleagues and organizing my routine.
            R – Result
            Within a short time, I became comfortable with both the corporate environment and city life, which helped me stay focused, productive, and confident at work.
